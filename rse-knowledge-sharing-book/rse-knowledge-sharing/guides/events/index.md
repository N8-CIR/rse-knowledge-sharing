## Key considerations

This is a WIP consolodation of some of the resources above

### Prior to Event

- Ensure plenty of warning
- Setup a  anonymous reporting mechanism
- Create name badges (You could even include a QR link to their linkedin page and the feedback form). have everybody's organisations and a space for pronouns.
- Create a quick comments feedback link
- Make sure to send directions

### During the event

- Providing options for dietary requirements in catering (e.g. vegan, halal, gluten-free). Follow up with the venue to check how they will action dietary requirements.
- Making provisions for audio/visual aids to cater for participants who may have hearing impairments.
  - This could include a notice at the presenter stand to remind them to check hearing aid equipment setup.
- Checking the physical accessibility of the venue for people with mobility impairments
- Offering translation or interpretation services for non-native speakers of the event's official language
- When presenting try to include audio descriptions of visual elements

### Accessibility

- Is the room or venue accessible for all attendees?  For example, is parking nearby and/or is disabled parking available?
- Is the speaker’s area accessible?
- Layout - boardroom style is most suitable for meetings
- Can seating be reserved for disabled attendees?
- Does the meeting room have a hearing loop?
- Have papers been circulated in advance?
- Are materials and resources available in accessible formats?
- Are breaks scheduled for longer meetings or events?
- Are presentations available in advance?
- Using the auto captioning functionality Google Meet and Zoom during online meetings and events. See [Virtual meetings(opens in a new tab)](https://docs.google.com/document/d/1EIuLiDXcqU6BMNE7U5x6h9pMsRXjKQuJKOQfryStlYY/edit) - captioning.
- Add details of accessibility to event info: Hearing loop, Online and in person,  Wheelchair accessible venue

## Other Ideas
